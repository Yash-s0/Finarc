Privacy Policy

Finarc Privacy Policy

Finarc is a local personal finance tracker. It is designed to keep financial records on your device and give you control over backups, exports, imports, notification detection, and data deletion.

Effective date: June 18, 2026 Offline-first Local-first No account required

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how Finarc handles information when you use the app. Finarc is a personal finance tracker only. It does not provide banking, lending, payment processing, investment, crypto, insurance, trading, money-transfer, or financial advice services.

Finarc does not require an account, login, backend, or cloud sync for normal app usage.

2. Data We Store Locally

Finarc stores personal finance records locally on your device so the app can provide tracking, summaries, reminders, and analytics. Depending on how you use the app, local records may include:

  • Accounts, cash wallets, Amazon Pay wallet entries, and balances.
  • Income, expense, transfer, adjustment, and reconciliation records.
  • Credit card records, billing cycles, dues, payments, refunds, and cashback.
  • Loans, EMI records, company deduction information, and related reminders.
  • Split expense groups, recoverables, people names, and partial recoveries.
  • Pending transactions created from user entry or optional notification detection.
  • Settings, detection preferences, alert preferences, and local diagnostics.

Finarc does not store CVV, card expiry, full card numbers, full bank account numbers, or bank credentials. Only masked or last-four card/account details may be stored.

3. Data We Do Not Collect

During normal app usage, Finarc does not collect or upload your data to a backend.

  • No account registration is required.
  • No backend or cloud sync is used during normal app usage.
  • No financial data is sold.
  • No financial data is shared with third parties by Finarc.
  • No bank credentials, CVV, full card number, or full bank account number are stored.
  • No banking, payment, lending, investment, trading, crypto, insurance, or advisory service is provided.

4. Notification Access Usage

Notification access is optional. If you grant notification access and enable detection settings, Finarc may inspect supported financial notifications locally on your device to detect transaction-like events.

Notification contents are processed on-device and are not uploaded to a backend during normal app usage. Chat, social, and email apps are blocked or ignored. UPI, payment, wallet, and CRED notification detection is optional and disabled by default unless you enable it.

5. Pending Transaction Confirmation

Detected notification events are not automatically committed as final finance records. They are added as pending transactions for your review. You must confirm, edit, or reject them before they affect your tracked balances and records.

6. SMS Permissions

The Play Store/release build of Finarc does not request or use SMS permissions. It does not request READ_SMS, does not request RECEIVE_SMS, and does not register an SMS receiver.

7. Local Storage and SQLite Database

Finarc stores app data in an on-device SQLite database. The database is local to your device and is used for app functionality such as tracking, summaries, analytics, reminders, pending transactions, backups, imports, exports, and reset actions.

The local SQLite database is plaintext. It is not currently encrypted by Finarc.

8. Backups, Exports, and Imports

Finarc provides user-controlled backup, export, restore, and import tools. Backup files and CSV exports are created only when you choose to create them. Finarc does not automatically transmit these files to a server.

If you import or restore a file, Finarc uses the file locally to update app data on your device.

9. Plaintext Backup and Export Warning

Backup files and CSV exports are plaintext user-controlled files. Anyone with access to those files may be able to read their contents. Store them privately, avoid sharing them through untrusted channels, and delete them when they are no longer needed.

10. Data Sharing

Finarc does not sell your personal data.

Finarc does not share your financial data with third parties and does not upload your app data to a backend during normal use. Data may leave your device only when you choose to export, back up, share, copy, move, or otherwise handle files outside the app.

11. Security Limitations

Finarc is designed as a local-first app, but local storage has limitations. The local database and exported files may be readable on compromised, rooted, poorly secured, or shared devices, or if you share exported files with someone else.

Protect your device with a secure lock screen and keep backups and exports in private storage.

12. Data Deletion and Reset

You can delete or reset local app data using Finarc's profile data controls or by uninstalling the app. Files you previously exported or backed up are outside the app's control and must be deleted separately from wherever you stored or shared them.

13. Children's Privacy

Finarc is not designed for children and is not intended for use by children.

14. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated when Finarc changes its features, storage behavior, permissions, or release behavior. The effective date at the top of this page should be updated when the policy changes.
